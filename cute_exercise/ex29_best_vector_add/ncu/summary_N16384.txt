SASS notes:

  torch.add uses scalar half loads/stores (`LDG.E.U16`, `STG.E.U16`) plus
  promoted math (`HADD2.F32`, `FFMA`) in its vectorized elementwise kernel.

  VectorAdd uses scalar 32-bit global loads/stores (`LDG.E`, `STG.E`) carrying
  two fp16 values per register and inline half2 math (`HFMA2`/`HADD2` in SASS).

Interpretation:

  The previous persistent v8 kernels had low achieved occupancy because they
  launched only a small multiple of SM-count CTAs. The one-tile scalar half2
  config launches the same 262144 CTAs as torch.add for N=16384 and has much
  lower register pressure, which is why it is the current graph-timing winner.
  NCU multi-pass duration still slightly favors torch.add, so CUDA graph replay
  remains the benchmark source of truth for the exercise timing target.
